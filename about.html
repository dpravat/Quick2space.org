Quick2Space is a newly-formed Washington nonprofit corporation, whose mission is to raise awareness and get people excited about space technologies by enabling small teams to build by themselves and participate in space-related experiments.

 

Small satellites, typically sized at 10x10x10 centimeters, are in high demand for education and research for running experiments in space. The CubeSats are the infrastructure in the small satellite in which the experiments are to be conducted.  It currently takes at least a year to build a typical CubeSat satellite.

Currently, it costs approximately $30,000 for students or researchers to work with a launch provider for a CubeSat launch and the process adds an additional 12-18 months to the timeline for the launch of a small satellite for an experiment.

 

Quick2Space plans to make it easier and cheaper to develop CubeSats by creating and building a set of open standards and open source components, thereby making it more accessible to students and scientists.  In addition, Quick2Space intends to work on refining the technology of building the CubeSats, in order to decrease the cost by half or more compared with commercial components. The lower cost to build and launch CubeSats will benefit students and scientists in conducting experiments in space or in high-altitude balloons.

 

In addition, the development, experimentation and launch of CubeSats will provide opportunities for Quick2Space to reach out to students in high school, college and graduate schools by involving them and educating them about space technologies. The programs it offers will include:

 

• Enabling student teams to launch and operate mini satellites in orbit through workshops and other educational events available to students and the general public.

• Encouraging innovation and competition by organizing hackathons and contests for launch opportunities.

• Helping students to participate in competitions in related areas such as robotics or science-related contests.

• Offering students opportunities to participate in mission design and launch through workshops or internship programs.
